Of Raisen, Madhya Pradesh

Raisen takes its name from the massive fort at the top of a hill. The town is located at the foot, Raisen was a strategic community in the history of eastern Malwa. It served as the 16th-century stronghold of Silhari, a Rajput (warrior caste) chief, and was an important administrative centre under the Mughals. Raisen lies in a region that was once part of Bhopal princely state.
